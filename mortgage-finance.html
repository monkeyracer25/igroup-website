THE CONTRACTOR MORTGAGE PROBLEM

Why Contractors Get Rejected

High street banks use automated systems designed for employed applicants. These systems fail to understand contractor income, leading to unnecessary rejections and frustration.

Income Misunderstood

Banks assess limited company directors on salary plus dividends, ignoring the true earnings potential of retained profits and consistent day rates that demonstrate real income far beyond what appears on a P60 or basic tax return.

Trading History Demands

Most high street lenders demand 2 to 3 years of accounts, which excludes newer contractors and those who have recently changed their trading structure from umbrella to limited company even if they have years of experience.

IR35 Confusion

Many lenders still do not understand how IR35 status affects contractor income. Some reject applications simply because the applicant is a contractor, regardless of their actual financial position or the strength of their income evidence.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Common Questions About Contractor Mortgages

Everything you need to know about getting a mortgage as a contractor. Our specialist team is always available to discuss your individual circumstances in detail.

Can I get a mortgage with less than 2 years of accounts?

Yes. Our specialist lenders can work with as little as one year of accounts, and in some cases even less. Your iGroup rate data and compliance passport provide the additional income evidence that lenders need to make a decision with confidence.

Do I need to use my salary and dividends as income?

Not necessarily. Some of our specialist lenders will assess your income based on your day rate multiplied by your working weeks, giving a much more accurate picture of your true earning capacity than salary plus dividends alone. This often means you can borrow significantly more.

Does IR35 status affect my mortgage application?

It can, but our brokers know exactly which lenders are comfortable with both inside and outside IR35 contractors. We match you with the right lender based on your specific IR35 position to maximise your chances of approval and the amount you can borrow.

How much does your mortgage service cost?

The initial consultation and assessment is completely free for iGroup members. Our mortgage brokers are paid by the lender on completion, so there is no upfront cost to you. Some specialist cases may carry a small broker fee, which will always be disclosed upfront before you proceed.

Can you help with remortgaging too?

Absolutely. Many contractors find themselves on poor rates because their existing lender does not offer competitive deals to self-employed borrowers. We regularly help members remortgage to better rates, often saving hundreds of pounds per month on their repayments.

What about buy-to-let mortgages for contractors?

Yes, we can help with buy-to-let applications as well. Many contractors build property portfolios as part of their long-term wealth strategy. Our brokers have access to specialist buy-to-let lenders who understand contractor income structures and can offer competitive rates.
