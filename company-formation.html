Three Reasons to Make the Move.

Most contractors start under an umbrella because it feels simpler. But when you run the numbers and weigh the options, a limited company wins every time. Here’s why.

Tax Efficiency

Keep more of what you earn. Limited company contractors typically take home 10–15% more than umbrella equivalents. That’s not a rounding error — at a £500/day rate, you could be looking at £8,000–£12,000 extra every year. The combination of salary and dividends, allowable expenses, and corporation tax rates creates a structure that rewards those who take ownership of their earnings.

Our team will walk you through the tax planning options available, so you understand exactly where the savings come from and how to maximise them legally and sustainably.

Professional Credibility

Clients and agencies prefer working with PSCs. A limited company signals commitment and professionalism. When you invoice through your own company, you’re not just another contractor — you’re a business partner. This distinction matters when negotiating rates, securing longer engagements, and building relationships with procurement teams who have strict vendor requirements.

Many of the UK’s largest employers require contractors to operate through a limited company. Having your PSC already established means you’re ready for any opportunity that comes your way.

Career Control

You’re the director. You choose your assignments, set your terms, and build equity in your own business. Unlike umbrella arrangements where you’re effectively an employee of the umbrella company, running a limited company means you have genuine autonomy. You decide when to work, what projects to take, and how to structure your income.

Over time, your company builds a trading history, a balance sheet, and a reputation. This becomes an asset — useful for mortgage applications, business loans, and demonstrating financial stability to clients and partners alike.

Five Steps. Five Days. Done.

We’ve refined this process over hundreds of company formations. Every step is designed to be as frictionless as possible, so you spend minutes, not hours, on admin.

1

Initial Consultation

We discuss your situation, your goals, and which company structure works best for you. Whether you’re a first-time contractor or transitioning from an umbrella arrangement, we’ll explain the options clearly. This typically takes 20–30 minutes and can be done over the phone or via video call. We’ll cover everything from company naming conventions and SIC codes to your planned trading structure and any existing commitments you need to work around.

2

Company Incorporation

We file with Companies House and your company is typically live within 24 hours. You’ll receive your certificate of incorporation, company number, and all founding documents. We handle the Memorandum and Articles of Association, appoint you as director and shareholder, and ensure everything is compliant from day one. If you have a preferred company name, we’ll check availability and reserve it for you during the consultation.

3

Bank Account Setup

Introduction to our preferred banking partners with fast-track contractor accounts. Opening a business bank account can be one of the most frustrating parts of setting up a limited company. We’ve partnered with banks that understand contractor businesses and can open accounts quickly, often within 48 hours. You’ll have a fully functional business current account ready to receive your first invoice payment.

4

HMRC Registration

Corporation Tax, VAT (if applicable), and PAYE registration — all handled by our team. We determine whether you need to be VAT registered based on your expected turnover and trading patterns, register you for Corporation Tax with HMRC, and set up your PAYE scheme so you can run payroll from day one. This step often catches people out when they try to do it themselves, but our team has the process down to a fine art.

5

Ready to Trade

Your company is set up, your accounts are open, and you’re ready to invoice your first client. At this point, you’ll have everything you need: a live company, a business bank account, all HMRC registrations in place, and a clear understanding of your obligations. We’ll also introduce you to the iGroup Compliance Passport, which keeps all your documentation current and audit-ready throughout the year.
